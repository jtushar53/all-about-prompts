# Clarity & Specificity

The single most impactful improvement you can make to any prompt is increasing its specificity. Vague prompts produce average outputs; specific prompts produce tailored ones.

Concretize every fuzzy noun or adjective. Replace "write something about X" with "write a 200-word explainer about X for a non-technical audience." Replace "improve this email" with "rewrite this email to be more concise, cut it to under 100 words, and end with a clear call to action."

Use numbers wherever possible: word counts, list lengths, rating scales, time limits. Numbers give the model a measurable target.

## Example prompt

```
You are a friendly science communicator.
Write a 150-word explanation of black holes for curious 10-year-olds.
Use at least one real-world analogy.
End with a one-sentence fun fact.
```
